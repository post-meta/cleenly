01

The two locked wordmarks.

CLEENLY ships two wordmark treatments. They are never mixed inside a single composition — each surface picks one. Primary wins on every product and chrome surface; editorial is reserved for closing moments and print.

Variant 05 · Primary CLEENLY
Primary · utility Marketing header, footer, app/PWA icon, booking topbar, partner badges, email header. Default for any product chrome. v05
Variant 11 · Editorial Cleenly
Secondary · editorial Email sign-offs, confirmation-page byline, pull-quote attribution, print copyright, business cards, mailing labels. Never on product chrome. v11

Both wordmarks share the same editorial contract: the secondary glyphs (EE and nly) carry the brand's italic single-word trick — same color, same weight relationship as <em> inside display headlines.

02

Anatomy of the primary mark.

Four parts. The CL and NLY are set in OPTIUniversSixtySeven at foreground; EE is set in Instrument Serif italic at foreground-soft. Letter-spacing is −0.02em — tight, not crowded.

Construction · Variant 05 CLEENLY
A · CL OPTIUniversSixtySeven 400 Foreground (#2D2826). Letter-spacing −0.02em. The utility face: mid-century sans, geometric.
B · EE Instrument Serif italic 400 Foreground-soft (#4A4540). Sits on the same baseline. Inherits the same color/weight contract as <em> in display copy.
C · NLY OPTIUniversSixtySeven 400 Matches A. Same face, same weight, same color. The italic EE is the only deviation.
D · Joins 2px optical gap each side of EE A 2-unit optical spacer prevents the serif italic from kerning into the geometric sans. Never collapse.
03

Clearspace.

Reserve one cap-height of empty space on every side of the wordmark. The cap-height of the OPTIUniversSixtySeven C is the unit (X). Nothing crosses this margin — not photography edges, not adjacent UI, not co-branding.

CLEENLY 1× 1× 1× 1×
Unit X = cap-height of C For a 64px wordmark, X ≈ 46px. Scale proportionally.
Minimum 1× on all four sides No element — type, image edge, button — enters the reserved frame.
Preferred 2× when space allows In editorial layout, double the margin. The brand reads as more confident when given room.
04

Sizing & legibility threshold.

The primary wordmark holds its italic EE moment down to 16px. Below that, the serif/sans contrast goes muddy on standard screens — drop to pure OPTIUniversSixtySeven CAPS (no italic EE) for favicons, status bars, and any usage under 14px.

64px CLEENLY Section headers · about-page mast
40px CLEENLY Marketing-site nav · default chrome size

Radius scales with size

Corner radius stays at 12% of the tile width. 16-radius at 128px; 4-radius at 32px. Never pure square, never circular.

One mark, never an alternate

The C is the only mark. Do not introduce monograms, droplets, sparkles, or house silhouettes. The wordmark carries the brand; the C carries the wordmark.

08

Misuse.

Six failure modes the wordmark survives without you. Don't stretch, squish, recolor the italic moment, italicize the rest, mix the two variants, or add effects.

CLEENLY
Don't · 01 Stretch the wordmark Never apply non-uniform scaling. Resize proportionally only.
CLEENLY
Don't · 02 Squish vertically Same rule. Aspect ratio is locked at the source proportions.
CLEENLY
Don't · 03 Recolor the EE in terracotta The italic EE stays at foreground-soft. Never accent-colored, never highlighted.
CLEENLY
Don't · 04 Italicize the whole mark Only the EE goes italic. CL and NLY remain upright OPTIUniversSixtySeven.
CLEEnly
Don't · 05 Mix v05 + v11 in one composition The two wordmarks never appear inside the same frame. Pick one per surface.
CLEENLY
Don't · 06 Add shadow, glow, or outline No effects. The wordmark sits flat on its surface. If contrast is poor, change the surface — not the mark.
