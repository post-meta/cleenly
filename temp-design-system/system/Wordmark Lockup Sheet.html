01

The one wordmark.

CLEENLY now ships a single locked wordmark: Cleenly set in Instrument Serif italic, title case, weight 400. It carries every surface — marketing, product, print, email. The previous primary/editorial split is retired.

Variant 03 · Locked Instrument Serif italic · 400 Cleenly
TypefaceInstrument Serif
Weight / style400 · italic
Tracking−0.035em

Why italic. The brand's editorial trick — wrap one word in <em> — moves from the chrome into the wordmark itself. The whole identity reads as one continuous editorial thought.

02

Anatomy.

Six letterforms. The capital C opens with a slight swash; the doubled e is the visual midpoint; the descending swash y is the signature. Italic angle sits at about 12°.

Construction · Variant 03
Cleenly
A · Capital C The cap-height anchor The opening swash sets the brand's mood — formal but soft. The C also becomes the mark on its own (see §06).
B · Doubled e Visual midpoint The double-e is what makes CLEENLY scannable. The italic kerning pulls them tight without ligaturing — keep both bowls distinct.
C · Swash y The signature descender The terminal y carries Instrument Serif's swash. This descender extends below the baseline — reserve space for it in every layout.
D · Italic angle ~12° from vertical Inherited from the typeface. Do not re-slant, re-skew, or apply any additional transform.
03

Clearspace.

Reserve one cap-height of empty space on every side of the wordmark — and measure the bottom from the descender of y, not from the baseline. Nothing crosses this margin.

Clearspace · 1× cap-height
Cleenly 1× 1× 1× 1×
Unit X = cap-height of C For an 84px wordmark, X ≈ 60px. Scale proportionally.
Bottom edge Measure from y descender Not from the baseline. The swash y is part of the mark.
Preferred 2× when space allows Editorial layouts breathe better at double. Italic needs more air than a roman wordmark.
04

Sizing & legibility threshold.

Instrument Serif italic holds beautifully at display sizes. Below 20px the swash terminals soften and the y descender starts to fight pixel rounding. Below 14px, switch to the upright C mark only — never set the full italic wordmark at favicon size.

16

Radius scales with size

Corner radius stays at ~14% of the tile width. 18-radius at 96px; 4-radius at 20px. Never pure square, never circular.

The italic C survives down to 16px

Below that the swash terminal disappears into a single pixel — but the silhouette still reads. Do not substitute a roman or sans C; the italic is the brand.

08

Misuse.

Six failure modes the wordmark survives without you. Don't unitalicize, stretch, bold, uppercase, recolor, or add effects.

Cleenly
Don't · 01 Make it upright The italic is non-negotiable. A roman Cleenly is not the brand.
Cleenly
Don't · 02 Stretch or squish Never apply non-uniform scaling. Resize proportionally only.
Cleenly
Don't · 03 Bold or semibold weight Weight 400 only. Instrument Serif at heavier weights changes the entire mood.
Cleenly
Don't · 04 All-caps or title-case ALL letters Only the C is capitalized. CLEENLY in caps reverts to a different brand era.
Cleenly
Don't · 05 Recolor in terracotta The wordmark is foreground (#2D2826) or paper (#F5EFE6) only. Accent on the wordmark is reserved.
Cleenly
Don't · 06 Shadow, glow, or outline No effects. The wordmark sits flat on its surface. If contrast is poor, change the surface — not the mark.
