*Collected 2026-06-14 for CLEENLY (Pro Craft Cleaning DBA) expansion evaluation.*
*Method: 6-angle deep-research harness — 28 sources fetched, 128 claims extracted, top 25 adversarially verified (3-vote), 21 confirmed / 4 refuted. Primary gov sources prioritized for regulatory claims.*

## Bottom line (BLUF)

**Enter via Vancouver, WA — not Portland, OR. Gate the whole thing on having a reliable local cleaner first.**

- The **market is attractive on demographics** (affluent, ~1M+ households) but **saturated, slow-growing, and offers no demand engine** for a zero-review newcomer. "Fast lead volume" is not realistic — think weeks-to-months and paid spend, not days.
- The **decisive finding is regulatory**: Vancouver WA is a low-friction extension of the *existing WA entity* (same DOR/UBI, a ~$53 city endorsement, no cleaning-specific license, WA's permissive 1099 rules we already use). **Portland OR is a different, riskier animal**: foreign-entity qualification, a 60-day registration trigger, and Oregon's employee-presumption regime that makes our 100%-contractor model **legally fragile**.
- Reframe: **Vancouver is not a "new market venture" — it's a cross-river extension of the same WA business.** That's what makes it cheap and sane. Portland is the venture, and it should be deferred behind Oregon counsel.

## 1. Demand — affluent, large, but slow-growing

**Portland–Vancouver–Hillsboro OR-WA metro (ACS 2024 1-yr, primary):**
- Population **2,537,070**, households **1,026,663**, median HH income **$98,994** (~20% above US median $81,604).
- Population growth only **~0.51% YoY** → demand expansion comes from *share capture*, not a growing pie.

**Vancouver / Clark County WA side (the part we'd actually serve first):**
- Clark County: **527,269** people, **202,253** households, median HH income **~$99,638** (~25% above US median).
- Vancouver city: **198,988** people, **85,374** households, median HH income **$82,928**. Next-largest Clark County cities (Camas ~26K, Battle Ground ~22K) are far smaller → **Vancouver is the dominant single-city target** on the WA side.

**vs Seattle metro:** Portland metro ≈ 2.54M vs Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue ≈ 4.0M population (Portland ≈ ⅔ of Seattle's size; Seattle figure is well-known but was *not* verified to primary source this run — treat as approximate). Median incomes are comparable; both are affluent, dual-income, high-homeownership markets where discretionary cleaning spend is normal. **Conclusion: demand is real and well-funded, but this is a "take share from incumbents" market, not a land-grab.**

## 2. Competitors — saturated, fragmented, with a predatory price anchor

- **National franchises don't blanket the metro.** Merry Maids runs **two distinct, locally-owned franchises** (Greater Portland out of Lake Oswego; West Portland/Hillsboro) with **non-overlapping territories and zero listed Vancouver WA coverage**. Textbook fragmented franchise coverage → geographic gaps exist (incl. Vancouver).
- **Marketplaces set a brutal first-touch anchor.** Homeaglow's Portland page advertises **"first cleaning for as low as $9"** (tiered $9/$19/$39 intro, ~82% off) funneling into a **$49–59/mo ForeverClean subscription** (corroborated by WBEZ, TINA.org/FTC complaint, BBB). A no-review newcomer competes against this on the cold open.
- **From the Seattle teardown:** **NW Maids already maintains a Portland location page** (Seattle/Kirkland/Tacoma/Portland) — Seattle tech-forward indies are *already* extending into Portland. We would not be first-mover among PNW operators.
- **Review moat: qualitatively strong, not quantified this run.** We did NOT verify specific GBP review counts/ratings of the top local players (see Open Questions). Plan assuming incumbents have hundreds of reviews and we start at zero.

⚠️ **Refuted — do NOT cite:** Homeaglow self-reporting "358 cleaners / 193 weekly cleanings" in Portland (0-3 refuted). Not a valid supply/demand signal.

## 3. Regulatory & operational entry cost — the headline asymmetry

### Vancouver, WA — low barrier (same WA entity)
- Requires only a **general city business license**, filed as a **city endorsement through the same WA DOR Business Licensing Service / UBI** the existing entity already uses. **No cleaning-specific license** — janitorial is explicitly just "ordinary business" under Vancouver Municipal Code Title 5.04.
- Fee tiers: **$0** (<$4k income) / **$53** / $347+$165/employee.
- **Pro Craft Cleaning DBA can add Vancouver activity through machinery it already operates.** (Minor: VMC Ch. 5.04 has a new version effective Jan 1, 2026 — verify fees at execution, no cleaning license added.)

### Portland, OR — higher barrier + structural risk
- **60-day registration trigger:** any profit-seeking activity, office, or employees/reps providing services in Portland/Multnomah County → must register a Revenue Division tax account within 60 days. Applies to a cross-state entrant. ($50K gross-receipts exemption affects *filing*, not the *registration* obligation.)
- **Oregon worker-classification regime (the real risk):** Oregon **presumes any worker who provides services for pay is an EMPLOYEE**; burden is on the business to prove independent-contractor status. Two tests apply in different domains — BOLI's **Economic Realities Test** (wage/hour, 6 factors) and **ORS 670.600** (tax/UI, conjunctive AND test incl. "customarily engaged in an independently established business"). For routine, business-integral cleaning labor, **nearly every factor leans employee.** Our WA-style **1099 overflow model carries real misclassification exposure in Oregon.**

⚠️ **Refuted — do NOT cite:** (a) an Oregon "Property Services Contractor License" requirement for janitorial labor contractors; (b) a sole-proprietor exemption to it; (c) the specific Portland tax stack "2.6% + 2% + 1%". The *worker-classification risk is real and well-sourced*; the specific licensing mechanism originally hypothesized was wrong.

> **This is not legal advice.** Confirm with Oregon counsel before structuring any Portland operation. WA-side Vancouver entry does not need this.

## 4. Fast-entry feasibility — "fast" is the wrong word

- **Google Local Services Ads (LSA) are gated.** All businesses must pass Google's **screening & verification before running** — ~**2–4 weeks** from document submission, and a brand-new, zero-footprint, contractor-staffed entity is the case **least** likely to be fast-tracked. A verified GBP is mandatory (Nov 2024 change). So **LSA cannot deliver an instant first lead.**
- **Organic GBP map-pack ranking** for a brand-new profile in home services was **not quantified to a cited source** this run, but industry consensus (uncited blogs) is months + review velocity. Assume the map pack is not a near-term channel.
- **Paid CPC / cost-per-lead for "house cleaning" in Portland: no primary data survived verification** (see Open Questions). We cannot budget the paid cold-start precisely yet — this is the single most important missing number.

**Realistic assessment:** With zero reviews, no established GBP, and a remote contractor crew, **meaningful lead volume is a weeks-to-months effort requiring paid acquisition and/or active review-building — not a quick switch-on.** The WA "GBP parasite" engine does **not** transfer.

## Risks (ranked)

1. **Operational (binding constraint):** a reliable cleaner 160–180 mi from the founders, with no on-site QA. 100% remote contractor ops = the marketplace model the brand deliberately rejected. **Nothing else matters until this is solved.**
2. **Oregon misclassification exposure** (Portland only) — defer behind counsel.
3. **Cold-start CAC unknown** — no verified CPL/CPC; could be uneconomic.
4. **Incumbent review moat + Homeaglow $9 anchor** — trust gap on day one.
5. **Bandwidth:** WA market itself is unvalidated (0 customer interviews) and core blockers (Twilio, Stripe, broken customer dashboard) are still open. Expansion competes for the same attention.

## Recommendation — phased, Vancouver-first, gated

**Phase A — now, ~free, reversible:**
- **Owner:** recruit **1–2 reliable Vancouver-area cleaners** (the long pole; everything gates on this).
- **Me:** prepare Vancouver + Clark County data for `lib/data/cities.ts` as a **draft, unpublished** (data entries + change the `state: 'WA'` type only if/when an OR city is ever added — Vancouver is WA, so even the type stays untouched). Cheap, reversible, ready for the green light.

**Phase B — gated on (cleaner secured + decision to spend):**
- File the **Vancouver city endorsement** on the existing WA UBI (~$53).
- Stand up a **Vancouver GBP** and start **LSA verification immediately** (2–4 wk clock).
- Publish the Vancouver/Clark County pages.

**Phase C — gated on data:**
- Small **paid test** once we know real CPL (resolve the open question first).
- **Hold Portland OR** until Vancouver proves out *and* we have Oregon counsel sign-off on worker classification.
